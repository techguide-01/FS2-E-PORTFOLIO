WEEK 4

Earthquake Drill

The fourth week of observation in Mr. Sunny Diche's TLE - Electrical Installation and Maintenance classes at Eulogio Rodriguez Integrated School brought a unique focus as the school conducted an earthquake drill. This week presented an opportunity to witness how the classroom environment and instructional routines adapted to ensure the safety and preparedness of both students and educators. In anticipation of the earthquake drill, there was a palpable sense of preparedness in Mr. Diche's class. Safety protocols were prominently displayed, and students were briefed on evacuation procedures and the designated assembly points. Mr. Diche seamlessly integrated discussions on electrical safety during emergencies, emphasizing the importance of shutting down electrical systems before evacuating. As the simulated earthquake alarm sounded, the class swiftly transitioned into action. Students calmly followed the practiced evacuation route, maintaining order and discipline throughout the process. The efficiency of the evacuation, coupled with the students' understanding of the importance of safety protocols, reflected the effectiveness of the school's preparedness initiatives. Following the earthquake drill, Mr. Diche led a post-drill discussion in the classroom. This session provided an opportunity to debrief on the evacuation process, address any concerns raised by students, and reinforce the importance of maintaining composure during emergency situations. Mr. Diche also connected this experience to the relevance of safety measures in the context of Electrical Installation and Maintenance. The earthquake drill served as a reminder of the broader commitment to safety within Mr. Diche's class. Throughout the week's lessons, safety considerations were seamlessly integrated into discussions on electrical systems. Students were encouraged to think critically about safety not only in routine activities but also in emergency situations, aligning with the comprehensive approach to safety within the TLE curriculum. Engaging with students after the earthquake drill provided insights into their reflections on the experience. Many expressed a heightened awareness of the importance of safety drills and felt more confident in their ability to respond effectively to emergencies. The integration of safety discussions within the TLE curriculum contributed to a holistic understanding of safety, encompassing both theoretical and practical aspects. The earthquake drill during the fourth week of observation at Eulogio Rodriguez Integrated School underscored the school's commitment to safety preparedness. Mr. Sunny Diche's class demonstrated a commendable level of organization and discipline during the drill, reflecting the efficacy of safety protocols. The seamless integration of safety discussions into the TLE curriculum further reinforced the importance of safety in both routine and emergency situations. As the observation period progresses, the experiences of this week contribute to a comprehensive understanding of the school's dedication to student well-being and the holistic education provided in Electrical Installation and Maintenance.

WEEK 6

Offset Bend

During the sixth week of observation in Mr. Sunny Diche's TLE - Electrical Installation and Maintenance classes at Eulogio Rodriguez Integrated School, a unique opportunity presented itself as I took on the role of the teacher. The focus of the week was the application of offset bend techniques in electrical conduit installations. This experience allowed for a hands-on exploration of teaching methodologies, student engagement, and the integration of practical skills into the curriculum. Preparation for the lesson on offset bends involved collaboration with Mr. Diche to align the content with the ongoing curriculum. The lesson plan included an overview of the importance of offset bends, the step-by-step process of creating offsets, and practical demonstrations. Visual aids, including diagrams and illustrations, were carefully selected to enhance understanding. As the teacher for the week, my approach was to create an interactive and engaging learning experience. I commenced the lesson with a brief review of previous topics, linking them to the current focus on offset bends. Students were encouraged to share their understanding of the concept, fostering a collaborative learning environment from the outset. A significant portion of the class was dedicated to a hands-on demonstration of offset bends. Utilizing PVC pipes, measuring tools, and bending equipment, I guided students through the process, highlighting key measurements, marking techniques, and the execution of bends. Emphasis was placed on precision and the impact of offset bends on conduit installations. To encourage active participation, students were provided with the opportunity to practice offset bends individually. I circulated among the students, offering guidance, answering questions, and providing feedback on their techniques. This interactive session allowed students to apply theoretical knowledge to practical scenarios, fostering a deeper understanding of the skill. Throughout the lesson, safety considerations were consistently emphasized. Students were reminded of the importance of using the correct tools, maintaining a safe workspace, and wearing appropriate protective gear. The integration of safety protocols into the teaching process aimed to instill a culture of safety consciousness in the context of electrical installations. Following the practical session, a post-lesson discussion was conducted. This allowed students to reflect on their experiences, ask questions, and seek clarification on any aspects of the offset bend process. The dialogue facilitated a deeper understanding of the topic and provided insights into areas that required additional reinforcement. The sixth week of observation, where I took on the role of the teacher to impart knowledge on offset bends, was a fulfilling experience. The collaborative approach with Mr. Diche, the emphasis on student engagement, and the integration of practical skills into the curriculum contributed to a dynamic and effective learning environment. As the observation period progresses, this week's experience enriches the overall understanding of the teaching and learning dynamics within the TLE - Electrical Installation and Maintenance class.

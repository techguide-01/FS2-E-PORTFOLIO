WEEK 3

Handling The Students' Quiz

During the third week of observation in Mr. Sunny Diche's TLE - Electrical Installation and Maintenance classes at Eulogio Rodriguez Integrated School, a unique opportunity arose as I took on the responsibility of handling the students' quiz. This experience provided a closer look at the assessment methods employed in the class, as well as insights into the students' understanding of the subject matter. In preparation for the quiz, collaboration with Mr. Diche was paramount to align the content and format with the ongoing lessons. We discussed the key concepts covered during the observation period and identified areas that warranted assessment. The quiz was designed to evaluate both theoretical knowledge and practical application of skills learned in the preceding weeks. On the designated day, I commenced the quiz with a brief review of the instructions, ensuring clarity on the expectations. The quiz encompassed a mix of multiple-choice questions, practical problem-solving scenarios, and short answer responses. The students demonstrated a focused and attentive demeanor, indicative of their commitment to the assessment process. As the students engaged in the quiz, I observed their approach to problem-solving and time management. The atmosphere was one of concentration, with occasional queries for clarification, which were promptly addressed. The diversity of question formats allowed students to showcase a comprehensive understanding of both the theoretical and practical aspects of Electrical Installation and Maintenance. Following the completion of the quiz, Mr. Diche and I engaged the students in a post-assessment discussion. This session provided an opportunity to address any lingering questions and clarify concepts that may have posed challenges. Mr. Diche encouraged an open dialogue, fostering an environment where students felt comfortable seeking clarification and understanding the rationale behind correct answers. Throughout the week, I had the opportunity to engage with students and gather feedback on their experiences with the quiz. The responses were generally positive, with students expressing appreciation for the balanced representation of topics and the alignment of the assessment with the class discussions. This feedback provided valuable insights into the effectiveness of the instructional methods and the students' perception of their own learning progress. Handling the students' quiz in Mr. Sunny Diche's TLE - Electrical Installation and Maintenance class during the third week of observation was a rewarding experience. The collaborative approach to quiz preparation, the focused and disciplined conduct of the students during the assessment, and the subsequent post-quiz discussion highlighted the effectiveness of the teaching and assessment strategies employed in the classroom. As the observation period continues, I look forward to further insights into the ongoing academic journey of both the students and their dedicated teacher.
